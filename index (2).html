GLARESAFE

An AI-powered device that reads traffic light colors through blinding glare — announcing the signal clearly so you can drive safely.

The Problem

When You Can't See the Light

Every driver knows the moment — sun directly in your eyes, dashboard reflection, or wet windshield scatter — when a traffic light turns into a blinding blob of light. You can't tell if it's red, yellow, or green.

This isn't just an inconvenience. Glare is a leading factor in intersection accidents, and existing solutions like sun visors and tinted glasses fail to selectively filter traffic signal glare. GlareSafe solves this with AI.

43% of drivers report glare significantly impairs their ability to read traffic signals at intersections (NHTSA data)

Solution

How GlareSafe Works

Four integrated stages take the guesswork out of glare — from raw signal capture to crystal-clear driver output in under a second.

🔭

Capture

An AI camera module equipped with a polarizing lens cuts through windshield and environmental glare to lock onto the traffic light.

🤖

Detect

Onboard AI vision classifies the traffic light color in real time — handling variable brightness, angle, and lighting conditions.

⚡

Process

The classification result is transmitted to an ESP32 microcontroller, which coordinates audio and visual output simultaneously.

🔊

Alert

The device speaks the detected color clearly — "Red," "Yellow," or "Green" — while a display confirms the signal visually.

Design Process

From Idea to Prototype

Phase 1 — Identify

Problem Discovery

The problem was identified through a real-world experience — a person directly experienced the danger of traffic light glare and brought it to us as an engineering challenge worth solving. We researched the scope of glare-related driving hazards and confirmed this as a genuine safety gap.

Phase 2 — Research

Technology Exploration

We researched existing solutions (polarized glasses, sun visors) and their limitations. We evaluated AI camera modules compatible with Arduino and ESP32 platforms, and studied how color-detection AI models work under variable lighting. The polarizing lens was identified as a key component to reduce glare before it reaches the sensor.

Phase 3 — Design

System Architecture

We designed the GlareSafe pipeline: polarizing lens → AI camera → microcontroller → dual output (audio + visual display). Component selection was driven by the $75 project budget constraint. We mapped input sensors and output channels to meet CIJE project requirements.

Phase 4 — Build

Prototype Development

Currently assembling the physical prototype. Components sourced; integration of the AI camera with ESP32 and audio output module in progress. The polarizing lens housing is being designed to mount securely for in-vehicle use.

Phase 5 — Test

Validation & Iteration

Next step: real-world testing under various glare conditions — direct sunlight, wet roads, night reflections. User feedback from drivers will drive iterative refinements to detection accuracy and output clarity.
